Resource Title

Be clear and concise

You are the expert of your own data. Help others find and understand your data by using clear, concise and simple language.

  • Limit use of technical language and acronyms.
  • Keep things short and to the point.
  • Use bullet point lists in place of long run on sentences.

Why primary and secondary tags?

Better search results! By splitting tags into primary and secondary options we can give different weight to the tags.

Which means if someone searchs for a particular tag the more relevant resources will show up first.

Primary tags should reflect the main point, purpose, or use of the data.

But we know there can be other useful information included in resources that may not be the primary focus. This is where secondary tags should be used.

For example a resource may primarily have data related to surface water availability, but also have some additional information about existing conditions and water quality. It may be entered as shown below:

  • Category: Surface Water; Planning
  • Primary Tags: Availability; Quantity
  • Secondary Tags: Quality; Location Description
